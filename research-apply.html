Research and Apply

  • Explore online job boards to find out possible listings for a potential job you might me intersted in.
  • Take a look and surf through websites like Indeed, Monster, and LinkedIn. Businesses and companies often post job listing straight onto those platforms, so it is important that you check those platforms regularly if you are looking for a job.
  • Check out local businesses in your area, such as restaurants, retail stores, or libraries where job listings might be posted. Local businesses and companies also often put up signs and posters, which are important to look out for.
  • Sometimes, you may get a job offer if you have taken OYAP or SHSM, and you are given an offer to work there. Keep an eye out for these types of offers if you are taking a specialized program like that.
  • Network with your friends, family, and teachers—they might know of open positions. Remember, networking is very important. If you happen to know teacher, who knows a kid who works at a place you also want to work at, the teacher and the other kid can work together and help you get a job there.
  • Be sure to read job descriptions carefully and only apply for jobs that match your skills and interests. Don't end up in a job that you hate or regret joining.
